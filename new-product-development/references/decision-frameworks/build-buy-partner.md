# Decision Framework: Build vs. Buy vs. Partner

Use this framework before committing to building any significant component.

---

## Decision Criteria

Score each option on five dimensions (1=poor, 5=excellent):

| Dimension | Build | Buy / License | Partner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Strategic differentiation | High — unique capability | Low — commodity | Medium |
| Time to market | Slow | Fast | Medium |
| Total cost (3yr TCO) | High upfront, low ongoing | Low upfront, recurring licence | Revenue share or JV cost |
| Control and flexibility | Full | Limited by vendor | Shared |
| Risk | High (execution risk) | Medium (vendor lock-in) | Medium (partner dependency) |

## When to Build
- Core competitive differentiator: this capability is what makes your product unique
- No acceptable off-the-shelf option exists
- Team has demonstrated capability in this domain
- Long-term strategic value justifies the investment

## When to Buy / License
- Commodity capability: authentication, payments, email, mapping
- Time to market is critical
- Cost of building > 2× cost of licensing over 3 years
- Vendor is established with clear roadmap and exit terms

## When to Partner
- Complementary capability: partner brings what you cannot build or buy
- Market access: partner provides distribution you cannot build organically
- Regulatory: partner has certifications that take years to acquire
- Build joint solution: combined offering is stronger than either alone

## Red Flags
- Single-vendor lock-in without contractual exit rights → negotiate data portability
- Open-source project with < 5 active contributors → assess fork-or-abandon risk
- Partner controls customer relationship entirely → protect your customer data rights

| Criterion | Weight | Score | Weighted |
|---|---|---|---|

Minimum acceptable total score: 3.5/5. Below 3.0 on data portability → escalate to legal before proceeding.

## Partner Agreement Minimum Terms
- Data ownership: your customer data remains yours
- Audit rights: right to audit partner's systems handling your data
- Exit clause: 90-day termination with data export
- SLA: defined uptime and support response commitments
- Exclusivity: define carefully; broad exclusivity limits future options
